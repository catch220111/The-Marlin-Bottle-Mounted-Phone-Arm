# The Marlin Arm

**A bottle-mounted folding MagSafe phone arm.**

A slim strap wraps around any standard water bottle. A nested, articulated arm unfolds to hold your phone at eye level — the filled bottle is the counterweight. No tripod, no desk, no extra gear: if you already carry a bottle, you already carry your filming rig.

Built for athletes and coaches who film their own training, creators who shoot in the gym / kitchen / outdoors, and anyone who wants a hands-free phone setup without extra equipment. Part of [The Marlin](https://themarlin.app) project by [Studio Prajna](https://studioprajna.com).

> ⚠️ **Status: early prototype (v0/v2 concept).** Nothing here has been print-validated yet. Dimensions, tolerances, and the hinge stack-up will change. We're looking for funding and early users to make this shareable and far more accurate — contact **piranha@studioprajna.com**.

## How it works (mechanical logic)

The v2 source encodes the actual engineering intent, not just shapes:

**Strap routing (belt logic).** The fixed end of the webbing loops through the spine bracket's lower slot and is sewn back on itself — the spine is *captured* by the strap, not glued. The free end wraps the bottle, returns through the upper slot, and is held by a low-profile tri-glide slider. Tension range fits **Ø83–95 mm bottles**.

**Hidden grip liner.** A 24 mm × 1.5 mm TPU liner — wider than the visible 15 mm webbing — is bonded to the strap's inner face under the spine. It spreads bearing pressure onto the bottle and provides the anti-rotation friction couple.

**Force path.**
`phone → MagSafe head → arm 3 → arm 2 → arm 1 → base pin → spine clevis ears → spine plate → TPU liner (friction × strap tension) → bottle wall` — and the bottle's mass is the counterweight against tipping.

**Nested hinge stack-up.** Each smaller arm's end sits inside the larger arm's channel, so the channel walls *are* the clevis — no extra knuckle hardware. Every joint, outside-in: shoulder-pin head | outer wall | friction washer | inner part | friction washer | outer wall | lock nut. 0.7 mm PTFE washers set the holding torque.

**Fold kinematics.** All three segments are modeled in the folded nest; the extended and deployed poses are generated by rotating the chain about the real pin axes, child-first. Pin axes are staggered 12 mm in X and offset in Z so adjacent pins never collide.

## Regenerating the models (`py/marlin_v2.py`)

The v2 source is a [CadQuery 2.x](https://github.com/CadQuery/cadquery) script. All seven STEP/STL configurations are generated from it.

```bash
pip install cadquery
# edit the OUT path near the top of the script first:
#   OUT = "/home/claude/marlin/out_v2"   ← change to your output dir
python py/marlin_v2.py
```

Key parameters live at the top of the script — strap length/width, segment span (`SEG_L`), channel widths for the three nested arms, pin radius, MagSafe plate/ring dimensions. Change a number, rerun, and all seven configurations re-export consistently.

### The v0 concept (`scad/themarlinwaterbottlev0.scad`)

The original sketch in OpenSCAD: a simpler 3-segment arm (3 × 100 mm) with ball-joint MagSafe head and an accessory loop on the strap. Kept for reference — open it in [OpenSCAD](https://openscad.org), tweak the hinge angles in the customizer to fold/unfold, and set `show_bottle = false` before exporting for print. **The v2 CadQuery model supersedes it.**

## Bill of materials (non-printed parts, v2)

| Part | Spec | Qty |
|------|------|-----|
| Webbing strap | 15 mm × 290 mm × 2 mm | 1 |
| TPU grip liner | 24 mm × 280 mm × 1.5 mm | 1 |
| Tri-glide slider | 15 mm, low profile | 1 |
| Shoulder pins | Ø7 mm (bore Ø3.2–4.2 spec) | 4 |
| PTFE friction washers | Ø12 mm OD × 0.7 mm | 8 |
| Rivets | Ø3 mm (strap + liner + spine tab) | 2 |
| MagSafe ring/magnet array | Ø56 mm OD | 1 |

Spine bracket: 6061 aluminium or glass-filled nylon. Arms: PLA is fine for prototyping; the channels print open-side-up without support at 0.2 mm layers.
